Tokyo Station is a blend of modern and historical architecture, making it a prime spot for architectural and street photography. Its iconic red brick facade, dating back to the early 20th century, contrasts beautifully with the surrounding skyscrapers, offering a mix of classic and contemporary elements. Early mornings and evenings provide great lighting for capturing the station's detailed structure and the bustling movement of commuters, adding a dynamic street photography element. Don't miss the Marunouchi side for its architectural grandeur or the Yaesu side for more modern urban views. Perfect for photographers interested in capturing the essence of Tokyo's history and city life.

Daikoku Parking Area, renowned among car enthusiasts, is a hotspot for automotive photography, especially during car meets. The area attracts a wide variety of vehicles, from vintage cars to high-performance sports cars, giving photographers an opportunity to capture diverse designs and details. The industrial backdrop of highways and overpasses enhances the dramatic effect of night shots, especially with the cars illuminated under the artificial lights. Ideal for those seeking to capture detailed close-ups of cars or wide-angle shots of the entire scene, this location offers endless creative possibilities for automotive photography.

The Izu Skyline is a breathtaking route that winds through the mountains, offering sweeping views of Mount Fuji, the Pacific Ocean, and lush forests. It's a haven for landscape photographers, with opportunities for both wide scenic shots and detailed nature photography. The curving roads also attract sports car and motorcycle enthusiasts, making it a great spot for sports photography. Sunrise and sunset add vibrant colors to the dramatic sceneries, while capturing vehicles in action against the backdrop of stunning landscapes offers a unique combination of speed and serenity. Ideal for those looking to combine natural beauty with dynamic motion in their shots.
